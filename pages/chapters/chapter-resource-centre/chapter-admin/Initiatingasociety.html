Initiating a Society

Setting up any society at university can be a challenging yet rewarding experience. Societies have many roles to play in the academic environment– they provide not only a route for like-

minded people to meet each other, but also an important source of new experiences for everyone involved. As the person initiating the society you are likely to experience all of these things from the word go Although setting up a society is not easy it can be a lot of fun and there are many places to go for support and advice. It is also immensely satisfying to see the society perform well.

People to contact:

  • Other students with an interest in setting up a Hindu society to form the initial committee
  • Contact Students Union society liaison about setting--‐up a society
  • Contact NHSF (UK) who can link you to nearby societies that can help out
  • The local Hindu Samaj for organizing cultural events and to provide continuing support

Rules/Guidelines to follow:

  • Follow guidelines of student union society set-up:
  • No. of members (minimum) and committee
  • Requirements Student Union member charge
  • Society election criteria

This will need to be done over summer to make sure that you are ready for freshers.

Events: Overview of preparation for the first events.

Freshers Fayre:

This is the most important time to get new members– if you miss fresher’s it is usually very difficult to get going as a society.

Make sure you have a stall there and take down interested people’s details. Often you can

get more committee members at fresher’s stalls as well

Initial Events:

Have first events advertised at fresher’s – with location and times already organised. This should be an intro meeting where students can meet each other and you can talk about activities planned (a few more activities should also be planned at regular intervals so that interest remains e.g. trips for Navratri, talks, socials etc – they do not have to be costly events)

Advertising:

On-campus::  Emails, text, phone, word of mouth, leaflets and posters.

Achieve contact with individuals verbally and in-person to attain maximum persuasion.

Finance:

  • Obtain information on financial assistance from the Student Union.
  • Be aware of the occurrence of Student Union finance meetings, and be in attendance.
  • Sponsorship: Obtaining support for the society from local individuals, businesses, and corporate firms in the funding of specific events or for general society funds.

Links and Communication:

  • Achieve links with student bodies, e.g. NUS
  • Student organisations/societies – Union of Jewish Students (UJS), Yoga, Sikh, Vegetarian
  • Student media– University newspaper/magazine, University wide emails,
  • Other NHSF Societies in your zone
  • Local mandirs and Hindu organisations

Strategy (event management, long-term planning & short term planning):

  • Create a timetable to work to:

Fulfil a plan through delegating work to a team.

Create a team ethic – team decisions mean accountability all round.

Timeframe – stick to a time frame in which to achieve certain goals.

  • Long-term planning: events timetable (variety), booking of halls, venue deposits, equipment hire, transport arrangements, entertainment bookings, and possible sponsorship. Give certain jobs such as these a longer period of time to put in place or for them to be confirmed.

  • Short-term planning: keep a short-term aim.  

Schedule to achieve small but important objectives in the run-up to an event. On the day/night of the event, have a strategy/timetable. It’s common to have a brief to run to, on the night of an event.

Examine planning/event priorities in order to keep to a timetable. If a different approach is needed, reassessing can be very important.

Long-term sustenance of society:

Need to make sure throughout the year that you find interested members to continue and expand the society next year. Therefore you should keep an eye out from the beginning for members who regularly attend events and you think have the right qualities to form the future committee
